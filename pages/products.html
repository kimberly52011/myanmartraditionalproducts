Burma's Products

Yoon

Traditional Myanmar lacquerware, or “Yoon ”, is one of the country's most famous handicrafts and has a very unique style. Yoon had existed mainly at Bagan, central part of Myanmar in 11th to 13th century A.D.Myanmar Lacquerware (Yoon) is widely used as kitchen ware, jewelry boxes souvenirs, home decorations and petitions. One of the oldest lacquers (yoon) was indeed discovered in the pagoda Mingalazedi, one of the built last with Bagan (at the 13th century).

Pathein umbrellas are special umbrellas made in Myanmar. They're colorful, durable, and often handmade with materials like cotton or silk and bamboo frames. They're famous for their intricate designs and are popular souvenirs for tourists

Shwe Che Toe

Myanmar's gold embroidery, Shwe Che Hto, has a thousand-year history originating in the Pyu era around AD 800. It's a royal decorative art found in clothing, accessories, and even as wall backdrops for affluent families, with Mandalay being its central hub. This intricate craft resembles painting in its decorative application, showcasing Myanmar's rich cultural heritage.
